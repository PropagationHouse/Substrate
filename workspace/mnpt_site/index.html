Intent-Driven Sparsity (IDS)

The Manifesto

We are entering an era where the fidelity of an AI model is no longer measured by how much it can generate, but by how little it needs to be told.
For years, the industry has obsessed over "Prompt Engineering"â€”the art of verbose, explicit instruction. We built elaborate scaffolds of text to constrain the chaos of early LLMs. We treated them like unruly genies that would twist our words if we didn't specify every clause and condition.
mnpt (Minimal Input) is the rejection of that fear. It is a protocol built on trust.

The Philosophy

The resolution of the input is inversely proportional to the shared context. As the agent's model of the user solidifies, the necessary bandwidth to trigger complex actions approaches zero.
This isn't about saving milliseconds. It's about flow state. In a high-velocity creative workflow, the friction of translating thought into English syntax is a tax. Every "Please" and "Can you" is a cognitive speed bump.
We are moving toward a Neural Interface without the hardware. We don't need brain-computer interfaces to achieve telepathy; we just need to stop over-explaining ourselves.
